Foil Pans, Lids & Sheets: What to Stock for Daily Service

A practical breakdown of the foil lineup every restaurant, deli, and caterer should keep on hand.

← Back to guides
Foil 4 min read

Aluminum foil products are one of the most consumed — and most often understocked — items in any commercial kitchen. Run out mid-service and you're improvising. Overstock and you're tying up cash in storage. The goal is a lean, consistent lineup that covers prep, holding, catering, and takeout without gaps.

Round pans vs. rectangular pans

The first decision is shape. Round pans are typically used for baked goods, rotisserie chicken, roasted vegetables, and portion plating. Rectangular pans are the workhorse of catering — lasagnas, rice, braised meats, and anything going into a steam table.

Round Pans — when to use

  • Baked goods, pies, cakes
  • Roasted chicken or proteins
  • Individual portion service
  • Retail display

Rectangular Pans — when to use

  • Catering and bulk service
  • Steam table inserts
  • Lasagnas, stews, rice
  • Takeout for large orders

Round pan sizes to know

We carry 7", 8", and 9" round aluminum pans, each with matching lids. The 8" and 9" are the most commonly ordered. If you're doing takeout for whole roasted chickens or pies, the 9" with a dome or foil board lid gives you good coverage without crushing the contents.

Rectangular pan series — 705, 747, 788

These three are our most-ordered rectangular pan lines. The 705 (1 lb) is the standard for single-meal takeout portions. The 747 (1.5 lb) is ideal for slightly larger portions or side dishes. The 788 (2.25 lb) covers family-size or bulk takeout. All three have matching plastic lids sold separately — order both together so you don't run out of one without the other.

Steam table pans — half vs. full, shallow vs. deep

If you run a buffet, catering operation, or large-scale food service, steam table pans are essential. The key decisions are size and depth.

  • Half medium pan — fits most standard chafing dishes, good for single side dishes or smaller mains
  • Full medium steam table pan — for high-volume items, soups, or bulk proteins
  • Full size deep pan — maximum capacity, used for large catering events
  • 768 series — a popular middle-ground pan that works for both prep and service, available as pan only or pan-and-lid set

Catering tip: Always order 10–15% more pans than you think you need for an event. They're lightweight, stack flat, and the cost of running short during service is much higher than having a few extra cases in your van.

Foil rolls and pre-cut sheets

Rolls are for wrapping, covering, and lining — they cut to any size you need. Sheets are faster for high-volume wrapping where every second matters. Both have their place.

Rolls

The 12"×1000 roll is the standard kitchen roll — good for covering pans, wrapping sandwiches, and general use. The 18"×500 wide roll is better for wrapping larger items or lining sheet pans. We also carry a 12"×1000 blue-labeled heavy-duty roll for applications where puncture resistance matters.

Pre-cut sheets

12"×10¾" foil sheets are ideal for delis and sandwich counters — no measuring, no cutting, just pull and wrap. They speed up service considerably during a rush and reduce waste from uneven cuts.

Lids: foil board vs. plastic

Foil board lids (like the 7" foil laminated board lid) are great for catering presentations — they look clean, hold heat well, and don't require matching a specific pan brand. Plastic lids snap on tighter and are better for transport where spillage is a risk. For steam table pans in a catering setup, foil board is standard. For restaurant takeout, plastic is usually safer.
